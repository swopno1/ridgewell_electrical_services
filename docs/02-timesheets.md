# Timesheet Logging

The **Timesheets** module is where you log your daily labor hours and keep a record of your work scope. This data is used for project tracking, payroll, and performance auditing.

---

## 1. Entering Your Daily Hours

Logging time is designed to be quick and straightforward. You should log your hours at the end of each shift to ensure accuracy.

1.  **Access the Form**: Navigate to **Timesheets** in the sidebar.
2.  **Select a Date**: Click on the date for which you want to log hours.
3.  **Enter Time Parameters**:
    *   **Time On**: The time you started work.
    *   **Time Off**: The time you finished work.
    *   **Break Duration**: The total minutes spent on unpaid breaks (e.g., 30 or 60).
4.  **Assign to Project**: Choose the correct **Client/Project** from the dropdown menu.
5.  **Scope of Work**: Provide a brief but descriptive summary of your tasks (e.g., "Installed new conduits in Section B; tested emergency lighting").
6.  **Submit**: Click **Submit Timesheet**.

> [!TIP]
> **Automatic Calculations**
> The system automatically calculates your **Total Hours** (Time Off - Time On - Break) and any **Overtime** hours if you exceed the daily threshold (typically 8.0 hours).

---

## 2. Managing Your History

You can review, edit, or delete your previous entries from the **Timesheet History** table below the entry form.

### Editing Entries
- Click the **Edit** (pencil) icon on any entry with a **PENDING** status.
- Update the necessary fields and click **Update Timesheet**.

### Deleting Entries
- Click the **Delete** (trash can) icon on a **PENDING** entry.
- Confirm the deletion in the dialog box.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Locked Entries**
> Once a timesheet entry has been **APPROVED** or **REJECTED** by a manager, it becomes locked. You cannot edit or delete locked entries; if corrections are needed, you must contact your manager.

---

## 3. Understanding Status Indicators

Each timesheet entry will display one of three statuses:

-   🟡 **PENDING**: The entry has been submitted and is awaiting manager review.
-   🟢 **APPROVED**: A manager has verified the hours, and they are now ready for payroll.
-   🔴 **REJECTED**: The entry was returned for correction. Check the **Feedback** column for notes from your manager.

---

## 4. Best Practices for Logging

To ensure smooth payroll processing and accurate project auditing:
- **Be Specific**: Avoid vague notes like "Worked on site." Use specific descriptors.
- **Log Daily**: Don't wait until the end of the week; logging daily reduces errors in time estimation.
- **Double-Check Projects**: Ensure you are billing time to the correct job to avoid administrative rework.
